Historical Places in India

Ram Setu

Ram Setu, is a chain of limestone shoals that connects the Pamban Island of India to Mannar Island in Sri Lanka. According to Hindu mythology, it is believed to have been built by Lord Rama and his army of monkeys to reach Lanka in the epic Ramayana. Ram Setu holds great religious and historical significance.

Konark Sun Temple

Konark Sun Temple, located in Odisha, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site dedicated to the Sun God. Built in the 13th century, the temple is known for its exquisite architecture and intricate stone carvings, depicting various aspects of Hindu mythology and daily life. It is designed in the shape of a gigantic chariot with intricately carved wheels and horses.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum located in Agra. It was built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Red Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort in Delhi. It was constructed by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan and served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors.

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Delhi. It is the tallest brick minaret in the world and is surrounded by several other historical structures.
